Setting Up Your Owl

Where to Put It

✓ Put it in the CENTER of your table

✓ Keep it 4-6 feet away from people

✓ Make sure nothing blocks it - it needs to see everyone

✓ Put it on a flat surface so it doesn't fall

✗ Don't put it near windows - too much light behind people

✗ Don't hide it in a corner - it can't see everyone

Tip: Think of it like a centerpiece on your table!

Plugging Everything In

Super Easy - Just 2 Steps!

Step 1: Plug in the Power

  • Take the power cord from the box
  • Plug the round end into the Owl
  • Plug the other end into the wall
  • The Owl will light up - that's good! ✓

Step 2: Connect to Your Computer

  • Take the USB cable from the box
  • Plug one end into the Owl
  • Plug the other end into your laptop
  • Wait 10 seconds - your computer will find it automatically!

Setup complete!

Joining a Zoom/Teams Meeting

3 Simple Clicks!

Before You Join Your Meeting:

  1. Open Zoom (or Teams, or Google Meet)
  2. Click the little gear icon (Settings)
  3. Find "Video" and choose "Meeting Owl 3"
  4. Find "Audio" and choose "Meeting Owl 3" for BOTH microphone AND speaker
  5. Close settings - you're ready!

Now Join Your Meeting:

  • Click your meeting link like normal
  • The Owl will automatically show everyone in the room!

Connecting 2 Owls Together

For Bigger Rooms or More Coverage

Why Use 2 Owls?

  • Large conference rooms - bigger than 18 feet
  • More than 10 people in the meeting
  • Better coverage - see more angles
  • Better sound pickup - covers whole room

How to Connect Them (Called "Owl Connect"):

  1. Download the Meeting Owl App on your phone
  2. Plug in BOTH Owls to power
  3. Connect ONE Owl to your computer with USB (this is the "main" one)
  4. Open the app and find both Owls
  5. Tap "Pair Devices" or "Owl Connect"
  6. Select both Owls you want to connect
  7. Wait for them to connect - they'll show as "Paired"

What Happens:

  • ✓ Both Owls work together wirelessly
  • ✓ They share video and audio automatically
  • ✓ Remote people see the best view from either Owl
  • ✓ No extra cables needed between the Owls!

Note: The phone app is required for pairing multiple Owls

Casting Your Screen to a TV

Share Your Meeting on a Bigger Screen

For Windows Users:

  1. Press Windows Key + P on your keyboard
  2. Choose "Duplicate" or "Extend" from the menu
  3. Then press Windows Key + K
  4. Wait for available devices to appear
  5. Click on your TV (like Roku) from the list
  6. Your screen will now appear on the TV!

For Roku TVs Specifically:

  • Make sure to press Windows + P FIRST
  • Select your display mode (Duplicate/Extend)
  • Then the Roku will appear when you press Windows + K

For Mac Users:

  1. Click the Control Center icon (top right)
  2. Click Screen Mirroring
  3. Select your Apple TV or AirPlay device
  4. Enter the code if prompted
  5. Your screen will mirror to the TV!

Alternative for Mac:

  • System Preferences → Displays
  • Click "AirPlay Display" dropdown
  • Choose your Apple TV or display
  • Select mirror or extend option

Note: Your TV must support wireless display (Miracast for Windows, AirPlay for Mac) or be connected via HDMI cable

Problems? Quick Fixes

"My computer doesn't see the Owl"

  • Check the USB cable is plugged in on both ends
  • Try a different USB port on your laptop
  • Unplug the Owl, wait 10 seconds, plug it back in

"People can't hear me clearly"

  • Sit closer - stay within 6 feet of the Owl
  • Close windows/doors to reduce outside noise
  • Check you're not muted in Zoom/Teams

"The video is blurry"

  • Wipe the camera lens with a soft cloth
  • Turn on more lights in the room
  • Check your internet connection

"I hear an echo"

  • Turn down the Owl's speaker volume
  • Make sure you only selected the Owl (not laptop speakers too)

Do's and Don'ts

DO These Things

  • Put Owl in center of table
  • Sit around it evenly
  • Keep it 4-6 feet from people
  • Turn on room lights
  • Speak at normal volume
  • Test before important meetings
  • Keep the lens clean
  • Close windows (less noise)

DON'T Do These

  • Put it in a corner
  • Block the camera view
  • Put it near windows
  • Sit in the dark
  • Shout at it
  • Touch it during meetings
  • Cover the microphones
  • Unplug it while it's on
